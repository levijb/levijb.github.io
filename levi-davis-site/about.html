About

Cognitive science, then data science, now both at once.

I'm a neuroimaging data scientist at Columbia University Irving Medical Center, where I build the pipelines, models, and infrastructure behind large-scale MRI research into Multiple Sclerosis and other neurodegenerative diseases. My work sits at the intersection of biomedical engineering, neuroscience, and machine learning — from processing raw DICOMs on an HPC cluster, to training deep learning models for lesion segmentation, to co-authoring the statistics behind a published paper.

Before neuroimaging, I taught myself 3D animation software to help visualize Alzheimer's progression for a documentary, and forecasted solar energy trends with time-series models — a reminder that I'm drawn to translating complex data into something people can actually see and understand, whatever the domain.

I grew up outside St. Louis, studied cognitive science and data science at the University of Virginia, and now live in New York City. I'm looking for my next role in neurotechnology, biomedical AI, or applied research — somewhere I can keep working at the boundary of basic science and real-world impact.
